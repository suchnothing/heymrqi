    • SPRING GUIDE

    General Advice

    1. This page will contain all of year 2, and will jump around a lot more. At this point, all that's left is to get those final few friends to max hearts, tie up some loose ends, and make a lot of money.
    2. The first time it rains pick up a marriage pendant from the weirdo near the beach tidepools and give it to the person you want to marry. Unless you're going the Krobus route, in which case as soon as he is at max hearts you can get his item from the Desert Trader and give it to him.
    3. The next time the book seller is in town, buy all the remaining special ability books, as well as the Queen of Sauce if it's available. If you can't get the cook book this time, get it the next time the book seller comes.
    4. Check your luck on the TV every day you're in in the Valley (don't worry about it on Ginger Island). When the TV tells you "The spirits are very happy today!" go to Calico Desert and open the casino by talking to the weirdo at the back of Sandy's shop. Play the slots until you get enough points to buy the alien rarecrow, and then return to whatever else you were going to do that day.

    Make Money

    Reaching perfection requires building all 4 warp obelisks and the golden clock, which costs a total of 13 million gold. This is the most challenging part of reaching perfection. Hopefully you're now well set up to rake in money so that you can buy the buildings before the end of year 2.
    1. Crops: Your most important money-maker is going to be crops. That's why you've crafted 20 iridium sprinklers. Plant rhubarb in Spring, starfruit in summer, and pumpkins in fall. Continue harvesting and replanting starfruit in your greenhouse across all seasons. Once you've grown all the special year 2 crops and you know you aren't missing anything from other seasons, you can start planting starfruit on that greenhouse sprinkler as well. Keep the ancient fruit sprinkler.
    2. Kegs: Increase the value of your crops by making as many of them as possible into wine or juice with the kegs. Every time you get more oak resin, make more kegs and slowly fill up empty space in your greenhouse, your farmhouse, and even your Ginger Island sleep hut if you run out space on the valley farm. Prioritize making starfruit wine, but when you run out of startfruit from the greenhouse you can use the crops you've grown outside. Always put the lowest quality in the kegs first, and when you get close to harvesting another batch of produce you can sell the high quality ones left over that you weren't able to put into the kegs.
    3. Truffles: Your 12 pigs should each produce 1 or 2 truffles each every day (unless it's raining). Make sure to pet the pigs and pick up the truffles as often as you can. You can make them into truffle oil if you want. However, I always choose the foraging profession that makes all foraged items iridium quality, and I find that the value increase for making iridium truffles into oil is so small that it's not worth the time. I just sell them as-is.
    4. Skull Cavern: Once you get Qi's challenge to reach level 100 of Skull Cavern on hard mode, you can make a lot of money by spending the entire day in the Cavern whenever you have nothing else going on by keeping the more difficult mode turned on. It makes the mines more dangerous, but it vastly improves the loot. You can get a ton of iridium and radioactive ore, which you can smelt into bars and sell. Combine that with the blacksmithing profession to increase their value. Monsters will sometimes drop prismatic shards and galaxy souls, which can be sold for a lot once you're done upgrading everything. You can get free Qi gems from giant slimes and exchange them for more galaxy souls to sell once you've finished buying everything you need from Qi's walnut room. And mummies will drop cloth and miners treats. Not to mention all the diamonds and other gems you find. You can often make 50-70k gold in a single day just from the Cavern. To make the Cavern a little easier, fully upgrade the galaxy sword using 3 galaxy souls, enchant it with Crusader so that you can kill mummies with your sword, and forge together an iridium band, luck ring, slime charmer ring, and burglars ring. Every time you spend a full day in the cavern, use lucky food and bombs to open up staircases and shafts quickly, and ignore gems and other loot for the first 30 floors or so. Just focus on getting down fast. The further down you go, the more iridium and radioactive ore you'll find, and those are the biggest money-makers from the Caver.
    5. Ancient Fruit: You should have (or hopefully soon have) a quality sprinkler with 8 ancient fruit plants around it in your greenhouse. Use those fruits to keep making more seeds, and slowly fill up all tillable spots on your Ginger Island farm. Eventually you'll transition from planting seasonal crops on the valley farm to entirely relying on ancient fruit crops on Ginger Island. They're perfect money-makers because they continuously regrow, requiring no maintenance, and they give fruit once per week on the exact same schedule as wine-making. Harvest the fruit, make wine. When you have enough ancient fruit planted to fill all your many kegs, you can abandon seasonal crops entirely (unless you want to plant them for fun). Maximize your output by using deluxe retaining soil on your ancient fruit so that they don't need to be watered. That way you can get rid of sprinklers and have more spaces for fruit. Ancient fruit takes an entire season to mature, so filling up your farm with a viable crop you can use for wine will take at least a few seasons.

    Great Friends

    You'll need to reach maximum hearts with everyone, so that's 8 hearts with dateable NPCs and 10 hearts with non-dateable. If you were able to follow the gift schedule in year 1, most of them should be full hearts already. There may be a few stragglers like Krobus and Dwarf, and of course Leo and Kent are met later in the game so their hearts will be very low.
    1. Kent: Don't miss Kent's birthday on Spring 4. Bring him a high quality rabbit's foot, or another loved item, for a quick jump in friendship points. When he asks you for a starfruit for his anniversary, bring him your highest quality starfruit right away as it will give another nice little jump in friendship. Try to give him 2 gifts per week and talk to him any time you pass him.
    2. Leo: Always carry one of your highest quality duck feathers when running around on Ginger Island in case you run into him. Once he moves to the Valley, make sure to go find him twice per week to give him a gift. His friendship will increase more slowly since his birthday is late in the year and he doesn't have any quests you can do for him. Just keep bringing him presents and you'll get there quickly enough.
    3. The Luau: If you got the Botanist profession you should be getting iridium quality truffles every day. Throw one in the Luau soup for an easy friendship boost to everyone left.
    4. The final stardrop: You'll need to marry someone or invite Krobus to be your roommate to get your last stardrop as their 12.5 heart event. If you're going the marriage route, go to the beach on the first rainy day of Spring (or any other rainy day) to buy the mermaid pendant from the creep in the bushes by the tidepools. If you're moving in with Krobus, you can get his item from the desert trader once Krobus reaches 10 hearts.

    Cooking

    You should hopefully have all the cooking ingredients you need stored up. The first day the bookseller is in town, go buy the Queen of Sauce cookbook. You'll still be missing a handful of recipes from friendship events or that need to be purchased. Figure out what you're missing and go get it if you can. Then, you can go to your kitchen and cook 1 of each recipe you haven't done yet. Personally, I like to wait until I get Qi's cooking challenge, because it gives a nice boost to completing it. But you don't have to do the cooking as part of the challenge if you don't want to.

    Crafting

    1. You're probably still missing the alien rarecrow. Check the fortune channel on the TV and when you get a day where you have very good luck, hit the casino. Bring some lucky food with you, too. You can find lucky lunch in mystery boxes, or save the one you make as part of cooking all the recipes. Although you can buy enough chips to get the rarecrow, it's much cheaper to max out your luck, just buy a few chips, and win it all at the slots. Bet the highest amount to increase your earnings faster. The day after you get the alien rarecrow you'll get a recipe for a new scarecrow in the mail.
    2. You may be missing some crafting recipes that need to be purchased. Visit Dwarf, Krobus, Robin, Pierre, Desert Trader, Island Trader, Mr. Qi, and Volcano Dwarf for all the purchased crafting recipes.
    3. Each level of mastery gives crafting recipes.
    4. You'll have to upgrade your house to have a cellar in order to get the cask recipe. Although it's an expensive upgrade, remember that it creates even more space for kegs.
    5. Keep fulfilling community board quests to get crafting recipes as rewards.

    Shipped Goods

    Your list of shipped items should be almost complete by now, but there are a handful of items you're still missing. You'll need to grow a mystic seed into a tree and tap it for mystic syrup. Hatch an ostrich and sell one of its eggs. Don't forget to ship a radioactive ore and a radioactive bar. And if you haven't done it yet, you need to ship one of each year 2 crop: garlic, cabbage, and artichoke.

    Spring 6, Saturday

    1. Craft a furnace and start smelting your copper ore.
    2. Craft a scarecrow and place it near your crops.
    3. Water your crops.
    4. Smelt some more copper.
    5. Pack your bag with: Sword, pickaxe, and parsnips and fish you have for the mines.
    6. If you didn't get the Community Center cutscene yesterday, go to town via the bus stop after 8am to trigger it and read the plaque on the floor.
    7. Go to the mines and collect more copper ore.
    8. Before you go to bed, put more copper in the smelter.

    Spring 7, Sunday (Lewis' Birthday)

    1. Smelt some more copper.
    2. Water your crops.
    3. If the copper you just put in the smelter is done, smelt some more before you leave.
    4. Pack your bag with: Fishing rod, hoe, geodes (don't forget to keep 1 frozen geode for the Community Center), museum donation items, and a gift for Lewis. Your highest quality parsnip is a fine gift for him.
    5. Check the traveling cart.
    6. Talk to the Wizard to gain the ability to read the junimo floor plaques.
    7. Give a gift to Lewis at his manor.
    8. Go to Clint’s and break open your geodes. Sell any doubles you don’t need to Clint. Donate museum items.
    9. Go back to your farm and put some more copper in the smelter.
    10. Put away extra items and plant any seeds you can, including any free seeds you got from the museum. If you have at least 50 sap in storage, make enough fertilizer to use on the seeds you just planted.
    11. Put some more copper in the smelter.
    12. Bring your fishing rod and hoe and fish for the rest of the day. If you are missing any fish that you need, go get them now. Then if it's rainy, fish at the town river for the rest of the day. If it's sunny fish at the lake until 7PM and fish at lv 20 of the mines after that (if you haven't reached lv 20 then just continue to fish at the lake).
    13. Put some more copper in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 8, Monday

    1. Put some copper in the smelter.
    2. Harvest your jazz and any other finished crops and water all the spaces.
    3. Put some copper in the smelter.
    4. Go to Pierre's and buy up to 4 tulip seeds (depending on space available) and enough parsnip to fill the rest of your empty spaces (if you have any).
    5. Plant your tulip seeds and fill the rest of the spaces with parsnip. Make fertilizer out of sap and fertilize the parsnip. If you have enough sap left for more fertilizer, also fertilize the tulips.
    6. Put some copper in the smelter.
    7. If it's rainy, fish at the town river for the rest of the day. If it's sunny, fish at the lake. Switch to level 20 of the mines at 7PM if you still need ghostfish, stonefish, or cave jelly (if you haven't reached lv 20 then just continue to fish at the lake).
    8. Keep your biggest stack of chub or smallmouth bass for the next time you go to the mines.
    9. Put some more copper in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 9, Tuesday

    1. Put some copper in the smelter. If you run out copper to smelt, start working on making refined quartz.
    2. A bunch of parsnip should be done. Keep it all for your checklists, to give as gifts, or to eat in the mines.
    3. Put some copper in the smelter.
    4. Chop enough wood for a new chest. Move all your community center, quest, and museum donation items into the new chest.
    5. Go to Pierre's and buy up to 4 tulip seeds and enough parsnip to fill the rest of your empty spaces (if you have any). Make sure to buy extra seeds to replace spots tomorrow as well. Go back to plant your seeds and store any extra. Use sap to make fertilizer and fertilize the spots you planted.
    6. Put some copper in the smelter.
    7. If it's rainy, fish at the town river for the rest of the day. If it's sunny, fish at the lake. Switch to level 20 of the mines at 7PM if you still need ghostfish, stonefish, or cave jelly (if you haven't reached lv 20 then just continue to fish at the lake). Keep your largest stack of chub or smallmouth bass to use as food in the mines so that you don't have to use as many parsnips.
    8. Put some more copper in the smelter before you go to bed.

    2. Water, harvest, and replant your crops.
    3. Pack your bag with: Hoe, regular bait, and a daffodil for Vincent. Also bring your fishing rod if you don't have enough to buy the iridium rod.

    5. If you started the day at fishing lv 6 and have 7500g, then bring your normal bait (save deluxe bait for legendary fish) and go buy the iridium rod and two trout soup from Willy. If not, skip buying the rod and get it the next time you go fishing.
      • On the first rainy day after you reach fishing 9, bring your deluxe bait and trout soup, craft a trap bobber, and go to the mountain lake and fish for the Legend. Save him to put him in a fish smoker later. Unless you reeeaaallly want to keep him for an aquarium, in which case you will have to find some other clever way to quickly make 10k in a single day.
    6. Pick up forage at the beach.
    7. If it's rainy, fish at the town river for the rest of the day. If it's sunny, fish at the lake. Switch to level 20 of the mines at 7PM if you still need ghostfish, stonefish, or cave jelly (if you haven't reached lv 20 then just continue to fish at the lake). Keep your largest stack of chub or smallmouth bass to use as food in the mines so that you don't have to use as many parsnips.
    8. Put some copper or quartz in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 11, Thursday

    1. Put some copper or quartz in the smelter.
    2. Harvest and water crops. Your beans, kale, tulips, and potatoes will be ready. Be sure to save one bean today for the community center. Ship one tulip and save the rest to gift to Evelyn as gifts. Save or sell the rest according to your checklist.
    3. Replant the tulips with more tulips. Plant the other spaces with carrot seeds you've collected, and pick up more parsnip seeds later to fill remaining spaces. Make fertilizer if you can and place it where the parsnips will go.
    4. Pack your bag with: Sword, pickaxe, hoe, and the fish you set aside for eating in the mines.
    5. Go to Cindersap forest and get Robin’s ax.
    6. Return the ax to Robin at her shop. Stop at Pierre’s on your way to Robin’s if you still have empty spots.
    7. Go as far as you can in the mines.
    8. If you got seeds at Pierre's earlier, plant them before you go to bed.
    9. Put some copper in the smelter before you go to bed.

    4. Pack your bag with: Fishing rod, hoe, geodes (remember to keep at least 1 frozen geode for the Community Center), and museum donations.
    5. Check the traveling cart, then pick up anything you need from Pierre’s (don’t forget to get extra to replace the cauliflower and whatever else will finish growing tomorrow).

    7. If it's rainy, fish at the town river for the rest of the day. If it's sunny, fish at the lake. Switch to level 20 of the mines at 7PM if you still need ghostfish, stonefish, or cave jelly (if you haven't reached lv 20 then just continue to fish at the lake). Keep your largest stack of chub or smallmouth bass to use as food in the mines so that you don't have to use as many parsnips.

    Spring 13, Saturday (Egg Festival)

    1. Put some copper in the smelter.
    2. Till and water 20 new crop spaces.
    3. Put some copper in the smelter.
    4. Harvest and replant your parsnips and anything else that's ready. Your cauliflower will be ready so keep one of the highest quality for a quest, sell one of the next highest quality, and keep the rest for cooking and the Community Center.
    5. Put some copper in the smelter.
    6. Clear trees, wood, and stone from your farm until 12 PM, using your mining fish to replenish energy if needed.
    7. Put some copper in the smelter.
    8. Pack your bag with the following community center donations: daffodil, wild horseradish, leek, dandelion, parsnip, bean, cauliflower, and potato.
    9. Go to the egg festival. Talk to everyone there (you can sneak around the back of the buildings to annoy Pierre), buy 20 strawberry seeds from Pierre, absolutely destroy those stupid children at the egg race, and put on your cute new hat.
    10. After you leave the festival, run right to the Community Center to donate the forage and crop items you have in your inventory and get the speed-gro fertilizer reward. Go right back to your farm and plant the strawberry seeds in the spaces you tilled earlier, and fertilize them with the speed-gro.
    11. Put the spring seeds in the shipping bin.
    12. Put some copper in the smelter before you go to bed.

Keep your largest stack of chub or smallmouth bass to use as food in the mines so that you don't have to use as many parsnips.

    Spring 15, Monday

    1. You should have some tulips and parsnips ready. Harvest and save them, and replant with any carrots or extra seeds you have. Water your crops.
    2. Pack your bag with: Sword, pickaxe, hoe, ax, 5 copper bars, and the fish you have set aside for the mines.
    3. Gather salmonberries as you pass by them. Save one of the highest quality you find, and ship one of the highest quality. Use the rest for energy or sell them. Don’t go out of your way to get them as they aren’t worth much.
    4. Buy 4 more tulip seeds from Pierre and enough parsnip to fill any remaining spots.
    5. Go to Clint's to upgrade your ax.
    6. Get as far as you can in the mines.
    7. Put some iron in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 16, Tuesday

    1. Put some iron in the smelter.
    2. Water, harvest, and replant your crops.
    3. Put some iron in the smelter.
    4. Pack your bag with: Sword, pickaxe, hoe, and food for the mines.
    5. Pick up seeds from Pierre's. You will likely have tulips and parsnip to replant tomorrow, so get 4 tulip seeds and as many parsnip seeds as you need.
    6. Get as far as you can in the mines, and if you've made it to the ice floors collect as much iron ore as you can. If you're having trouble with the harder mobs on the ice levels, try to going back to lower levels for a bit and killing bugs until you beat the monster slayer goal, then go get your new weapon from Gil and return to the ice floors.
    7. Put some iron in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 17, Wednesday

    1. Put some iron in the smelter.
    2. Water, harvest, and replant your crops.
    3. Pack your bag with: Hoe, river jelly, sea jelly, cave jelly, up to 10 hardwood.
    4. If you have 5 iron bars (or enough iron to keep smelting up to 5 bars) ready for tomorrow, also pack your fishing rod and your extra coal (you should keep at least 35 coal in your crafting chest right now). If you don't have enough iron, then instead pack your sword and pickaxe.
    5. Put some copper in the smelter.
    6. Pick up your ax from Clint.
    7. Buy the fish smoker recipe from Willy.
    8. Pick up forage items on the beach.
    9. Go back to your farm and chop up to 10 hardwood to craft the fish smoker if you don't already have enough.
    10. Put some copper in the smelter.
    11. Either go fishing or mining depending on your iron situation. If you go fishing, put the smoker down nearby and smoke the highest quality fish you catch to increase their value when sold (smoke catfish or largemouth bass, don't waste coal on anything else).
    12. If you've caught Legend, smoke him and check "Smoked Fish" off your checklist. If not, do this when you catch him. Don't sell him until you've reached Fishing lv 10 and have the angler profession.
    13. Put some copper in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 22, Monday

    8. Plant seeds and put quartz in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 24, Wednesday (Flower Dance)

    1. Put some quartz in the smelters.
    2. Make 2 more preserves jars with wood you collect today. Preserve as many normal quality strawberries as you can, save silver and gold to give as gifts, and sell any remaining normal quality you have.
    3. Water, harvest, and plant crops. Today is your last day to plant parsnip.
    4. Put some quartz in the smelters.
    5. Clear wood until 12PM.
    6. Put some quartz in the smelters.
    7. Attend the flower dance and talk to everyone. Make sure to buy the rarecrow and Barrel o' Flowers recipe from Pierre. Watch the weird dance.
    8. Put some quartz in the smelter.
    9. Chop wood until bed time.
    10. Put some iron in the smelters.

    Spring 25, Thursday

    1. Harvest and water remaining crops. Today is your last day to plant carrot.
    2. Pack your bag with: Fishing rod, hoe, 498 wood, 10 hardwood, 99 stone, and 5 gold star parsnips. If you have a clam, cockle, mussel, oyster, and crab, bring those too. If you don't have them all, keep trying to collect them and bring them the next time you visit the community center.
    3. Go to the Community Center and donate the 5 gold parsnips. Complete the Construction bundle in the crafts room, as well as the entire Vault Room. You should hopefully have money for it enough now. It costs $42,000g in total. If you don't have enough, complete the vault as soon as you reach $42,000 to unlock the bus. Complete the crab pot bundle if you have all the donations.
    4. Unlock the bridge at the beach and pick up forage.
    5. Put the crab pots somewhere convenient and fill them with bait. From now on, every time you pass by empty the crab pots and replace the bait.
    6. Go back to your farm. Sell the chocolate cake, save the fertilizer, and place the lightning rod and crystalarium on your farm. Put a jade in the crystalarium.
    7. Pack your bag with: Sword, pickaxe, hoe, and food for the mines.
    8. Get as far as you can in the mines.
    9. Put some gold in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 26, Friday (Pierre's Birthday)

    1. Put some gold in the smelter.
    2. This should be your last bean harvest of the season so there is no need to continue watering your bean plants. Keep watering the strawberries and any crops that aren't done yet.
    3. Check the fruit bat cave.
    4. Pack your bag with: Fishing rod, hoe, and a normal (or silver if you have a lot) quality parsnip for Pierre.
    5. Check the traveling cart.
    6. Look for morel in the secret woods.
    7. Give the parsnip to Pierre for his birthday.
    8. If it's rainy, fish at the town river for the rest of the day. If it's sunny fish at the lake. If you haven't got an ice pip, switch to lv 60 of the mines after 7PM. Smoke the ice pip before you sell it. Keep your largest stack of chub or smallmouth bass to use as food in the mines so that you don't have to use as many parsnips.
    9. Put some gold in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 27, Saturday (Emily's Birthday)

    1. Put some gold in the smelter.
    2. Water strawberries and crops that aren't done yet, and harvest anything that's ready.
    3. Collect and sell strawberry jelly.
    4. Do the community vault donations today if you weren’t able to earlier.
    5. Pack your bag with: Fishing rod, hoe, and an emerald or ruby for Emily.
    6. Give Emily her gift at her house after 12PM, or at the Saloon after 4PM.
    7. If it's rainy, fish at the town river for the rest of the day. If it's sunny fish at the lake. If you didn't get an ice pip, switch to lv 60 of the mines after 7PM. You can also switch to mining if you're running low on fish bait, coal, or ores.Keep your largest stack of chub or smallmouth bass to use as food in the mines so that you don't have to use as many parsnips.
    8. Put some gold in the smelter before you go to bed.

    Spring 28, Sunday

    1. Put some gold in the smelter.
    2. Collect final harvest of strawberries and like before put as many normal quality as you can in preserve jars, keep silver and gold quality ones, and sell the rest.
    3. Don't water anything.
    4. Pack your bag with: Fishing rod, hoe, and your highest quality daffodil. If you aren't confident in your fishing skills, also craft a trap bobber to help with the scorpion carp.
    5. Check the traveling cart.
    6. Look for morel in the secret woods if you still need some.
    7. If you have at least 20,000 gold, go purchase the Big Chest recipe from Robin, and make 3 of them to replace your crafting, donation, and general chests. Keep the small chests for later. If you don't have enough wood to make them all, make as many as you can and do the rest later.
    8. If you were able to repair the bus, go to the Calico desert.
    9. Pick up any coconuts and cactus fruits and save them.
    10. Give the daffodil to Sandy and purchase 15 starfruit seeds (6,000g). You should have gotten 1 starfruit seed from Gunther at this point, so add that to make 16 total.
    11. Go fishing at the desert pond until you catch a scorpionfish and at least 2 sandfish, then return to the valley. Keep all the sandfish you caught and sell the scorpionfish.
    12. Put some gold in the smelter.
    13. Place one of each of the following items in the shipping bin and check them off the checklist: Sap, clay, copper bar, iron bar, copper ore, iron ore, coal, wood, stone, slime, bat wing, fiber, and refined quartz. You will eventually need 85 bat wings, so after you collect that many you can sell any others you find.
    14. Craft the Wild Seeds (Sp) out of the forage items you saved. Crafting it once makes multiple seeds, so keep 2 of them in your crafting chest and sell the rest. Also craft a field snack. You can eat or sell the field snack.
    15. Pack your bag with: Sword, pickaxe, hoe, and mine food.
    16. Go to the mines for the rest of the day.
    17. Put some gold in the smelter before you go to bed.
